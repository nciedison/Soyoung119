About Edison

The company was originally founded in 1899 as a Brahm stalker who wrote one of the famous novels 'Dracula'. Since he published Dracula he wanted to run his own publishing company. He had a mission to publish books for writers who were unable to do so themselves. Stoker and his family were passionate about supporting other writers financially and providing them with advice.


After Stoker's death in 1912, the company faced financial difficulties and was unable to pay its employees. However, with the help of writers who donated money to the company, Edison was able to recover from the crisis.


Our retail shops

Today, Edison has over 45 retail stores in Ireland, with its largest store located at Mallor Street Lower, Dublin 1 International Financial Services Centre. The store boasts a large conference room where writers and celebrities can meet customers, as well as spacious relaxation areas where customers can socialize, and share ideas. In 1995, Edison established an online store, making it possible for customers worldwide to access quality books. The company has also secured exclusive contracts with various authors, ensuring a diverse and compelling selection of literature.


Social contribution

  • Contributing to charities

  • Educational support
